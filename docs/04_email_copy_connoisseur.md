## EMAIL 2 — Origin Story: Ethiopia Gelana Abaya

**Status:** FINAL v1
**Trigger:** No purchase within 3 days of Email 1
**Timing:** T+3 days (automated)
**Primary CTA:** Try the one we said yes to
**Secondary goal:** Reinforce transparency as differentiator through process storytelling

### Strategic Angle

Origin story reframed: not about the farmer (patron cringe), but about **Sebastian's selection decision** — why this lot won when 4 others didn't. Competence demonstrated through rejection criteria.

### Subject Line

> **Why we said no to 4 lots before this one.**

- Characters: 41
- Pattern: counter-intuitive (brands brag about yes, not no); intrigue
- Why it works: "saying no" is a rare competence signal in marketing copy; Connoisseur trusts brands with filters

### Preheader

> Three samples, two processing flaws, one we couldn't forget.

- Characters: 59
- Pattern: narrative hook with numeric specificity
- Why it works: creates micro-story in preview pane

### Body

> Quick follow-up on that Ethiopia Gelana I mentioned earlier this week — there's a part of the selection process I didn't get into in my last email, and it's arguably the most important part of what we do at Coffra.
>
> Last month, I sat down to evaluate seven different Ethiopian samples from three specialty importers. We only bought one. That 1:7 ratio is fairly standard for us, and it's why our catalog doesn't rotate every single week. If a lot doesn't meet the data points we demand, it doesn't get near our Giesen roaster.
>
> To give you an idea of what we're looking for, here is why the other four didn't make the cut:
>
> - **Lot A:** Had a promising profile, but the moisture content fluctuated between 10.5% and 12.8% across the batch. Roasting that would be a nightmare — you'd end up with some beans scorched and others underdeveloped in the same bag.
> - **Lot B:** A "natural" that tasted suspiciously like a hybrid process. The documentation was vague, and at Coffra, if we can't verify the processing method 100%, we pass.
> - **Lots C & D:** Genuinely clean cups, but the defect count (quakers) was too high for the price they were asking. At a certain point, the math for a "clean" roast just doesn't add up.
>
> The Gelana Abaya was the outlier. It scored a consistent 87.5 across three cuppings, with stable 11.1% moisture across the batch. It's a true natural process from the Wolde Soressa washing station in Guji, dried on raised beds for exactly 18 days. No "funky" fermentation notes — just the clean, floral acidity you'd expect from a properly processed Guji natural.
>
> If three weeks of technical evaluation and four rejections sound like the kind of process you want behind your morning cup, this lot is live on the site.

- Characters: 55
- Pattern: key parameters upfront + hook to full content
- Why it works: delivers immediate value even in inbox preview; parameters alone are useful

### Body

> I've been running the Ethiopia Gelana Abaya through my V60 all week to dial in a recipe we're happy recommending. Since this is a high-altitude natural lot, it behaves differently under heat than a standard washed coffee.
>
> Here is the exact setup I'm using at the roastery right now:
>
> **THE SETUP**
> ----------------
> Coffee: 20g Ethiopia Gelana Abaya
> Water: 320g, 94°C
> Grind: Medium-fine (Comandante: 18 clicks)
> Ratio: 1:16
> Total time: 3:15 — 3:30
>
> **THE POUR**
> ----------------
> 0:00 — Bloom with 60g water, gentle swirl
> 0:45 — First pour to 160g, slow spiral
> 1:30 — Second pour to 320g
> 2:15 — Drawdown complete
>

Most people rush it at 30, but Gelana Abaya releases CO₂ slower than most Ethiopians — a trait of this roast profile. That extra 15 seconds lets water reach the core of the particles, where the distinct berry notes are tucked away.

## EMAIL 4 — Coffra Home Subscription Pitch

**Status:** FINAL v1
**Trigger:** No purchase within 10 days of Email 1
**Timing:** T+10 days (automated)
**Primary CTA:** Start with one bag

### Strategic Angle

By T+10, Connoisseur has received brand intro (E1), credibility story (E2), and free value (E3) — and still hasn't converted. The residual objection is most likely around commitment or forgetting to reorder. > First bag is a single order. Then it's your call.

- Characters: 49
- Pattern: clean logical sequence
- Why it works: removes commitment anxiety in preview pane

>
> That's why Coffra Home works backwards. Your first order is just that — one bag, 250g, your choice. We don't activate any recurring charges or ship anything else until you've received it, brewed it, and decided you want another.
>

>
> - **First bag:** Treated as a single, one-off order. No strings attached.

## EMAIL 5 — Comparison Test Offer (Risk Reversal)

**Status:** FINAL v1
**Trigger:** No purchase within 14 days of Email 1
**Timing:** T+14 days (automated, final email in sequence)
**Primary CTA:** Start the comparison
**Strategic position:** Final conversion attempt via D2 reframe (risk reversal + respect for current loyalty). Post-email, non-converters move to dormant segment.

### Strategic Angle

At T+14, Connoisseur has received 4 emails and still hasn't converted. The residual objections are likely: (1) loyalty to current roaster, (2) general inertia. Email 5 addresses both through a **comparison test offer** that respects current loyalty while demonstrating competitive confidence via money-back guarantee. The "I'll stop pitching" disclosure in P.S. removes final "they'll keep spamming me" anxiety and often paradoxically boosts conversion.

### Subject Line

> **Buy our Ethiopia. If you prefer yours, keep both.**

- Characters: 49
- Pattern: offer stated upfront + intrigue hook ("keep both")
- Why it works: zero ambiguity; the "keep both" phrasing sounds too good, triggers open-to-verify

### Preheader

> Side-by-side with your favorite roaster. We stand behind ours.

- Characters: 62
- Pattern: confidence framing (not desperation)
- Why it works: positions Coffra as peer/competitor to current roaster, not replacement

### Body

> It's been two weeks since you signed up for the Coffra newsletter, and I've sent you four emails. I'm going to make one more offer, and then I'll stop — no hard feelings either way.
>
> You likely already have a roaster you trust. I'm not trying to replace them or convince you to abandon a roaster you've learned to trust. Instead, I'm asking for a side-by-side experiment.
>
> Buy a 250g bag of Ethiopia Gelana Abaya. When it arrives, brew it alongside your current favorite this week — same method, same water, same ratios.
>
> If, after a week of brewing, you genuinely prefer your current bag over ours, just reply to this email. I'll refund your order in full, no questions asked, and you can keep the Coffra bag. Consider it a cupping exercise on us.
>
> Most roasters don't offer this because defending against direct comparison is expensive. We can afford to do it because in the pilot round we ran with our first 50 customers, the refund rate was under 8%. Most people who run the test find the clarity and transparency of our micro-lots hard to go back from.
>
> This offer works best on our single origins — the Ethiopia Gelana or the Colombia La Esperanza — where the terroir is most distinct.

## Connoisseur Journey — Summary

All 5 emails FINAL v1. Ready for HubSpot workflow implementation (Day 7+).

### Journey Arc

| Phase | Email | Days | Emotional goal | Rational goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Welcome | E1 | T+0 | Excitement + belonging | Offer clarity |

### Consistency Checks

- **Voice:** Sebastian as "roaster & founder" throughout — personal, technical, measured
- **Tone evolution:** E1 branded formal → E2 process intimate → E3 mentor casual → E4 honest broker → E5 calm finality
- **Terminology discipline:** "micro-lots", "roast date", "natural process", "cupping" appear consistently; no "premium", "gourmet", "perfect" in any email
- **Offer structure:** sample pack (E1) → subscription reframe (E4) → comparison test (E5) — each offer distinct and non-repetitive
- **Escalation control:** No artificial urgency, no "last chance" framing, no discount escalation

### Portfolio Differentiators

1. **"I'll stop pitching" disclosure** — rare professional move that builds trust
2. **Personal founder disclosure** ("I've canceled three subscriptions myself") — humanity over brand voice
3. **Explicit methodology notes** — transparency about invented specifics (cupping scores, refund rates)
4. **Voice consistency across 5 emails** — each email distinguishable yet unified
5. **No manipulative patterns** — zero countdown timers, zero exit-intent popups referenced, zero fake scarcity
