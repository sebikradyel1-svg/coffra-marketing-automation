## EMAIL 1 — Welcome + Free Sample Pack Offer

**Status:** ✅ FINAL v1
**Trigger:** Newsletter signup (website form, QR code in-store, checkout opt-in)
**Timing:** T+0 (automated, immediate)
**Primary CTA:** Shop fresh coffee
**Secondary goal:** Set brand tone (technical, specific, transparent)

### Subject Line

> **Ethiopia Gelana just landed. You're in.**

- Characters: 39
- Pattern: specific product name + insider framing ("you're in")
- Why it works: differentiates from "Welcome, here's your discount" generic openers; signals specialty coffee knowledge immediately

### Preheader

> Ethiopia Gelana Abaya. 1850m. Naturally processed. Try 80g on us.

- Characters: 64
- Pattern: technical signals (altitude, process) + offer teaser
- Why it works: Connoisseur recognizes "1850m" and "naturally processed" as credibility markers

### Body

> The Ethiopia Gelana Abaya lot arrived from the Guji zone two weeks ago, and it hit our roasting drums in Timișoara yesterday.
>
> Most coffee in Romania sits 8+ weeks between roast and cup. This one was roasted 24 hours ago.
>
> Coffra was started with a simple rule: we never touch lots larger than 500kg, and we print the exact roast date on every single bag. Precision isn't a marketing angle for us — it's the baseline. When you work with micro-lots, there's no room for "approximate" roasting.
>
> Here's the offer, no strings: on your first order over 150 RON, we're adding a Discovery Sample Pack (3 × 80g) to your box at no extra cost. Ethiopia Gelana, Colombia La Esperanza, and the Signature Blend — calibrate your palate across the current lineup.
>
> Start with a single 250g bag or stock up for the month. The samples are yours either way. No codes, no timers — just fresh coffee delivered when you're ready.

### CTA Button

**Text:** `Shop fresh coffee →`
**Destination:** `/collections/whole-beans` (product collection, not homepage)

### Sign-off

> — Sebastian
> Roaster & Founder, Coffra

### P.S.

> P.S. If you're wondering how we keep a 250g bag of high-altitude Ethiopia at 100–120 RON, the answer is logistics. By sourcing smaller, direct lots, we maintain healthy margins without the typical specialty coffee markup. Full cost breakdown on the site.

### Technical Specs

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Subject length | 39 chars (well under 50 limit) |
| Preheader length | 64 chars (within 40-70 sweet spot) |
| Body word count | ~220 words |
| Reading time | ~55 seconds |
| Hero image | [To be AI-generated — Ethiopia lot + roast date tag] |
| Mobile preview test | ✅ Subject + preheader render fully on Gmail/iOS |

### Words Used (Connoisseur approved)

- Technical: **Guji zone, roasting drums, micro-lots, roast date, altitude, naturally processed, high-altitude**
- Industry: **lot, batch, calibrate, palate, profile**
- Trust signals: **exact, baseline, no room for "approximate"**

### Words Avoided

- ❌ Premium, gourmet, perfect, amazing, best
- ❌ "Crafted with love", "our passion", "wake up to the aroma"
- ❌ "Limited time", "hurry", "don't miss"
- ❌ Excessive exclamation marks

### Copy Decisions Log

| Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Full EN (not RO mix) | Connoisseur consumes global coffee content in EN — tone is native |
| Specific stat "8+ weeks" | Concrete comparison > vague superlatives |
| "24 hours ago" roast date | Hyper-specific time = credibility signal |
| No discount code | Connoisseur distrusts discount-heavy brands; sample pack > % off |
| Sebastian named as founder | Human trust > "The Coffra Team" |
| P.S. cost transparency | P.S. has higher read rate than body; use for differentiator |
| CTA to collection, not home | Reduce friction to purchase decision |
